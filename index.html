Move fast and make things.

The world needs more makers.

In the extreme, consumerism is a form of learned helplessness. In consumer culture, making is something we've forgotten we can do. It has pushed us from the mainstream to the margins. However, there's something else available: We can see ourselves as producers. In maker culture, we define ourselves based on what we can do, and what we can learn to do.
- Dale Dougherty

I learned to code working in local government web development and quickly became convinced that the tech sector was out of touch with the needs of government. I then started at a school of government only to learn the feeling was mutual.

This website and the content in it are an effort to flex my “maker” muscles as I learn what we can do to use technology for public good.
