Parrots, Inc.

I currently serve as the COO and design lead for an extremely cool company called Parrots, Inc.

At Parrots, I get to use my design skills to help fine tune an artificial intelligence-powered robotics solution to help those with paraplegia speak to caretakers and loved ones. Our solution was features on the Today Show, was a top pickin the biotech category at Tech Crunch, and is currently in testing at a number of hospitals and care facilities around the country. We're proud to be a part of the Verizon 5G Challenge.

COVID Safe Paths

In 2020 I served as the founding product and design lead for Path Checks, a nonprofit spun out of MIT focused on developing privacy-preserving contact tracing solutions for the COVID-19 pandemic. Our team of over 600 volunteers is composed of a consortium of epidemiologists, engineers, data scientists, digital privacy evangelists, professors and researchers, all committed to flattening the curve of COVID-19, reducing fear, and preventing a surveillance-state response to the pandemic.

We have developed both web and mobile applications for use by individuals, governments, and public health officials. This app's UI has drawn on the talents of many designers, especially those from Upstatement volunteers.

Maps, Measles, and Media

This project was completed during January of 2020 as part of the #CodeforPolicy2020 course under FiveThirtyEight's Dhrumil Mehta. A joint project with four students from both the Kennedy School of Government and Chan School of Public Health, we used the "have fun, go make cool data visualizations" prompt to create this PSA about the prevalence of measles in the U.S. and globally. The entire course was a whirlwhind 2 weeks from start to finish and ran scrum-style, serving as both a data visualization and programming bootcamp as well as a crash course in agile.

The project made use of twitter APIs, datawrapper and python-backed D3 visualizations, and github for version control.
